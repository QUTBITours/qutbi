Types of Australia Visas

Visitor Visa (Subclass 600)

For tourism, visiting family/friends, or short-term business activities.

Valid for up to 12 months with multiple entries.

Electronic Travel Authority (Subclass 601)

For short-term tourism or business visits.

Available to eligible passport holders via the Australian ETA app.

Business Visitor Visa

For attending conferences, negotiations, or short-term business activities.

Typically valid for up to 3 months.

Required Documents

Passport

Valid passport with at least 6 months validity beyond intended stay.

All old passports, if available.

Photographs

2 recent color photographs (35x45 mm) with white background.

Visa Application Form

Completed online via ImmiAccount on the official Australian immigration website.

Visa Fee Payment Receipt

Proof of payment for the visa application fee.

Travel Itinerary

Flight bookings and accommodation details.

Additional Supporting Documents

Financial Documents

  • Bank statements (last 6 months)
  • IT returns / Form 16 (last 2 years)
  • Proof of income / salary slips

Employment Documents

  • Employment letter or business registration documents
  • Leave approval letter from employer

If Invited by Australian Resident

  • Invitation letter
  • Copy of invitee's passport and visa/residency proof
  • Address and contact details

Visa Application Process

Step 1

Create ImmiAccount

Register on the official Australian immigration website to start your application.

Step 2

Complete Application Form

Fill in the visa application form with accurate details and upload required documents.

Step 3

Pay Visa Fee

Make the payment for the visa application fee using a valid payment method.

Step 4

Submit Application

Review all information and submit your application for processing.

Note: Processing times may vary. It's advisable to apply well in advance of your intended travel date.

Important Guidelines

Health and Character Requirements

Applicants must meet health and character requirements. Medical examinations may be required.

Proof of Funds

Demonstrate sufficient funds to cover your stay in Australia.

Travel Insurance

It's recommended to have travel insurance covering medical emergencies during your stay.

Accurate Information

Ensure all information provided is accurate and truthful to avoid visa refusal.
